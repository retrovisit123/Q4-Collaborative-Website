Soil Aeriation


What is Spike Aeriation?

Think of Spike Aeration as a "quick refresh" for your lawn. This process involves using solid, wedge-shaped tines to poke holes directly into the soil. Unlike core aeration (which removes plugs of dirt), spike aeration creates pathways without leaving messy soil clumps on your grass.

The Key Benefits

  • Instant Nutrient Boost: Gets fertilizer and water to the roots faster.
  • Improved Drainage: Helps prevent water from pooling on the surface after a light rain.
  • No Mess: Since no soil cores are pulled up, your lawn stays looking clean and manicured.
  • Perfect for Maintenance: An excellent choice for lawns with mild soil compaction.

What is Core Aeriation

Think of Core Aeration as a "deep tissue massage" for your lawn. This process uses a machine to physically remove small plugs of soil, creating "breathing room" for your grass to thrive.

The Key Benefits

  • Fixes Compaction: The best solution for hard, high-traffic soil.
  • De-Thatching: Naturally breaks down the "suffocating" layer of debris.
  • Drought Resistance: Encourages roots to grow deeper and stronger.
  • Nutrient Highway: Opens a direct path for air and water to reach the roots.

What is Liquid Aeration?

Think of Liquid Aeration as a "liquid solution" for your lawn's breathing problems. It creates microscopic pathways in the dirt, loosening compaction without the need for heavy machinery.

The Key Benefits

  • Total Coverage: Reaches every square inch of your soil.
  • Zero Mess: No soil plugs or dirt clumps left behind.
  • Utility Safe: No risk of hitting sprinkler lines or cables.
  • Deep Action: Penetrates further than mechanical tines.
